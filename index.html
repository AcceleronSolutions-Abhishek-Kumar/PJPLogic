How PJP Works — End-to-End

A Permanent Journey Plan is a structured, algorithm-driven weekly visit schedule for each sales role. It ensures every rep visits the right dealer, influencer, or distributor at the right frequency based on business conditions.

01 What is a PJP and who gets one? Both Teams ▶

A PJP is an AI-generated visit schedule showing who to visit, when, in what order, and why. It is created at the beginning of every week for the coming week, covering approximately 6 planned visits per day per field rep.

The following roles receive a PJP:

SO (Primary) → FE (Secondary) → ASM → ATM → RSM (suggested) → SH / TH (suggested)
RSM, SH, and TH cannot be given a PJP — they receive a suggested PJP which they can approve or modify. Their unplanned visit slots are used for joint field visits with SO/FE.
02 End-to-end flow: from data to visit schedule Both Teams ▶
1. Data Inputs Collected
YTD lifting per dealer, MTD achievement, OS (outstanding), secondary liquidation, influencer activity (i0–i3 status), lead assignments from helpline, market type classification for the SO's geography.
2. Market Type Determined
Geography is classified as High Penetration (40+ dealers), Penetrated (20+), Under Penetrated (10–20), Severely Under Penetrated (<10), or Vacant. This sets the baseline split between existing vs. prospective dealer visits.
3. Dealers / Influencers Ranked
Each dealer gets a composite score from 4–5 Impact Factors (past performance, MTD achievement, secondary, OS, influencer activity). Weighted by market type. Rank order is produced.
4. Visit Limits Applied
Min and max visits per dealer per month are checked against dealer category (A–H) and total dealer count. Cap is enforced — no dealer can be visited beyond the ceiling even if ranked high.
5. Route Optimized
Route built from starting point. Max 100 km intra-city, 250 km upcountry. Lower-ranked dealers can be inserted (up to 5 positions down) to minimize travel distance while maintaining business priority.
6. Weekly PJP Generated
~70–80 planned visits distributed across the week (6/day). 2–4 unplanned visit slots kept open daily. PJP is delivered to SO/FE at end of previous week for next week.
03 Seasonal recalibration of dealer ranks Both Teams ▶

Dealer ranking uses 2 business seasons, not the full calendar year. This prevents dealers from being penalized or boosted unfairly by off-season performance:

Season 1: Jan – Jun | Season 2: Jul – Dec

Performance metrics are evaluated within the current season window, resetting at season boundaries. This recalibration affects the Impact Score calculations.

Primary — Market Type Classification

The single most important input to a Primary SO's PJP is what type of market their geography is. This determines how visits are split between existing and prospective dealers.

M1 5 market types and what they mean Primary ▶

Each SO geography is classified based on active dealer count. This drives the entire visit budget for the month:

Key insight: In Vacant markets, 100% of visits are on prospective dealers. In High Penetration, 80–85% are on existing dealers. The system automatically shifts effort toward expansion as penetration drops.
M2 Fortnightly evaluation — not monthly Primary ▶

The entire market matrix is re-evaluated every 15 days, not monthly. This means each monthly cycle produces two planning rounds:

Days 1–15: ~70–80 planned visits then Days 16–30: ~70–80 planned visits

Each cycle re-ranks dealers based on freshly updated data. A dealer who billed in Week 1 may fall in priority for Week 3 if MTD is already on track, freeing up visits for laggards.

Primary — Priority Scoring Formula

Every dealer gets a composite priority score from up to 5 impact factors. The score determines visit sequence within the monthly budget.

Score = A × Impact1 + B × Impact2 + C × Impact3 + D × Impact4 + E × Impact5

Penetrated Market: A=25% B=25% C=25% D=25% E=0%
All Other Markets: A=10% B=30% C=25% D=15% E=20%
I1 Impact 1 — Past 6-month primary performance Primary ▶

Weight: 25% (Penetrated) / 10% (Others). Looks at historical volume trend — declining dealers get deprioritized for recovery visits.

HO/Senior assigned → Declining T-3>T-2>T-1 → 0-billing since T-3 → NOD declining → Rest
I2 Impact 2 — MTD critical triggers (current month) Primary ▶

Weight: 25% (Penetrated) / 30% (Others). Highest weight in non-penetrated markets. Fires on current-month danger signals.

CRM issues raised → MTD <70% pro-rated → <15 MT in last 15 days → Active but not billed in 15 days
I3 Impact 3 — Secondary/liquidation performance Primary ▶

Weight: 25% all markets. Rewards dealers with strong secondary movement — signals genuine sell-through, not just primary offtake.

20 MT+ secondary (last 15d) → 10 MT+ secondary (last 15d) → 1 MT+ secondary → Rest
I4 Impact 4 — Outstanding collection control Primary ▶

Weight: 25% (Penetrated) / 15% (Others). Targets dealers with overdue outstanding to prompt collection.

Inactive + OS>30d or Net OS≥60% → Active dealers

OS priority cascade: i0 → i1 → i2 → i3 (i0 = most overdue, most urgent)

I5 Impact 5 — Influencer visits (Engineers, PCs, Masons) Both Teams ▶

Weight: 20% (Non-Penetrated markets only). Applies to BOTH Primary and Secondary teams. Visits on brand influencers who drive recommendations to end customers.

High-End Eng. >45d not visited → Rest High-End Eng. → High-End PCs/Masons >45d → Active Engineers → Active PCs/Masons

Primary — Prospective Dealer Prioritization

New dealers not yet onboarded are ranked using a separate priority chain focused on potential size, recency of last visit, and competitive opportunity.

P1 Prospective dealer visit priority order Primary ▶

Prospective dealers are ranked separately from existing ones. The system ensures new market development happens systematically, not just when SOs have spare time.

1. Sales-team flagged repeat visit
A rep has manually flagged this prospective dealer as requiring a follow-up. Highest priority — human override.
2. Large potential, not visited in 45+ days
High-potential dealer that has gone cold. Urgency of re-engagement.
3. Large potential (recently visited)
Large dealer still in active nurturing but within 45-day window.
4. Medium potential, not visited in 45+ days
Same logic as step 2, applied to mid-size dealers.
5. Medium potential (recently visited)
6. YTD degrowing competitor dealers within 5 km
Dealers currently buying from competition who are losing volume — highest conversion probability.
7. Inactive dealers within 5 km
Previously onboarded but gone dark, within geographic reach.
8. Small potential / Rest

Secondary — FE PJP: 3 Pillars

Unlike Primary which is dealer-first, Secondary FE PJP is structured around three distinct objectives that must all be balanced every week.

Pillar 1
PJP Assignment Maximization
All valid helpline leads (Hot & Warm) are auto-assigned for next 7 days and must be fulfilled first, before standard priority logic kicks in.
Pillar 2
Lead Generation Maximization
Prioritize Engineers, PCs & Masons using a priority ranking to maximize secondary volume leads. Re-evaluated every weekend.
Pillar 3
Intra-month Weekly Objectives
Weeks 1–2 focus on non-working influencer re-engagement. Weeks 3–4 shift to dealer support and working influencers.

Secondary — Influencer Priority Logic

Engineers and PCs/Masons are ranked every weekend for the coming week. High-end and Low-end tiers have the same priority chain but different "rest" treatments.

E1 Engineer priority (High-End: Chairman's, Diamond, Platinum, Gold) Secondary ▶
Meet attendee (last 7d) → Sales-flagged → Active, not visited in 45d → Active rest → i0 → i1 → i2 → i3
E2 Engineer priority (Low-End: Silver, Bronze, Inactive, Prospective) Secondary ▶
Meet attendee (last 7d) → Sales-flagged → Active, 45d not visited → Active rest → i0→i1→i2 → Newly onboarded prev. month → Prosp. not visited in 1 month → i3 + rest
D1 Dealer priority for Secondary FE Secondary ▶

Dealer ranking uses 50% past 6-month performance + 50% MTD performance (different from Primary's 4-5 factor model).

Past 6-month chain:

HO/Senior assigned → Declining T-3>T-2>T-1 → 0-billing since T-3 → NOD declining → Rest

MTD performance chain:

Billed >15 MT in last 15d → Inactive with stock >20 MT → Rest

Secondary — ATM & TH Visit Rules

ATM and Technical Head (TH) have fixed weekly visit targets split across engineers, PCs/Masons, and channel. They cannot be given a binding PJP.

TH visit priorities: Engineers up to Platinum, PCs up to Champion, then distributor → degrowing large dealer → inactive large dealer → active medium.

ATM visit priorities: Engineers up to Gold, PCs up to Superstar, then distributor → degrowing large + medium → inactive large.

AT2 District-level prioritization for ATM & TH Secondary ▶

TH: Top 3 districts by volume (till last month) → Focus district of current month → rest

ATM: Top 2 districts by volume (till last month) → Focus district of current month → rest

ATMs and THs get their Meet visits (min 4/month each) auto-assigned to their PJP for upcoming weeks, before other slots are filled.
